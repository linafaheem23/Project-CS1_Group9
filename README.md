Main Idea:
The ER Command Center is a desktop application that simulates how a real hospital emergency room manages its patient queue. Instead of a manual or paper-based system, this app digitally tracks every patient from the moment they arrive, automatically ranks them by medical urgency, and helps staff decide who gets treated first. The goal is to reduce wait times, prevent critical patients from being overlooked, and give staff a clear live overview of the ER floor at all times.

Features
User Roles & Access

Three roles: Doctor, Nurse, and Patient — each with their own login and dashboard
Patients register using a unique ID assigned by the nurse at intake

Patient Management

Nurses fill out an intake form with the patient's name, age, symptoms, heart rate, SpO2, and blood pressure
Each patient gets an automatic triage score based on their vitals — the score increases the longer they wait
The live queue displays patients ranked by urgency with color coding: red for Critical, orange for Urgent, gray for Normal

For Doctors

One-click button to assign treatment to the highest-priority patient
System automatically discharges the patient after treatment and frees up the bed and doctor
Simulation mode that auto-generates patients every few seconds for testing

For Patients

Patients can log in and see their current status, triage score, and estimated wait time

Analytics & Reporting

Tracks available beds and doctors in real time
Records average wait times by priority category
Exports a full shift report as a CSV file
Data is saved to local files and reloaded automatically on next launch
